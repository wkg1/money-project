About AI Tools Review

Honest reviews, real testing, no pay-for-placement. We help you find the best AI tools for your workflow.

Our Mission

The AI tools market is overwhelming. Every week, a new AI writing assistant, image generator, or video platform launches, each claiming to be revolutionary. Most review sites are either surface-level feature lists copied from the tool's own marketing page, or they are designed to push you toward the tool with the highest affiliate commission.

AI Tools Review exists to solve this problem. We test every tool ourselves, with real-world tasks, and publish honest comparisons that actually help you decide. Our revenue comes from affiliate commissions when readers choose to purchase through our links — but our recommendations are never influenced by affiliate relationships. We often recommend free tools, open-source tools, or tools with no affiliate program at all, because our first commitment is to being useful to you.

How We Test AI Tools

Every tool on this site has been tested personally by our team. We do not publish reviews based on demos, marketing materials, or second-hand reports. Our testing methodology varies by category but always follows these principles:

  1. Real-world tasks. We use each tool for the same tasks a real user would. For writing tools, that means blog posts, ad copy, emails, and social content. For image generators, it means generating images across multiple styles and use cases.
  2. Identical prompts. We use the same prompts across all tools in a comparison so the results are directly comparable.
  3. Multiple reviewers. At least two team members score each output independently. We average the scores to reduce individual bias.
  4. Paid plans. We test on paid plans, not free tiers, because that is the experience most serious users will have.
  5. Regular re-testing. AI tools update frequently. We revisit our comparisons every 3-6 months and update scores, pricing, and recommendations when things change.

Editorial Independence

We do not accept payment for reviews. No tool company can pay to be included in our comparisons, to receive a higher score, or to have negative feedback removed. If a tool has a weakness, we say so plainly. We believe this is the only way to maintain reader trust over the long term.

Some AI tool companies have offered us early access to features, extended trials, or briefing calls with their product teams. We accept these opportunities because they help us understand the tools better, but they do not influence our evaluations. If a company gives us something of significant value (such as a free annual subscription for testing), we disclose that in the relevant review.

How We Make Money

Transparency about our business model is important. Here is exactly how AI Tools Review generates revenue:

  • Affiliate commissions (primary). When a reader clicks an affiliate link and purchases a subscription, we earn a commission. Commission rates vary by program, typically ranging from 20% to 40% of the first payment (monthly or annual). We do not control these rates.
  • No sponsored content. We do not publish sponsored posts, paid placements, or "partner content." Every article on this site is written by us, based on our testing.
  • No display ads. We do not run Google AdSense or any other display advertising that would clutter the site or compromise the reading experience.

This model means we only succeed if readers trust our recommendations and find them valuable enough to purchase through our links. That alignment of incentives is deliberate — we designed our business so that the best thing for our readers is also the best thing for us.
