### LISTING PROMPT 1: The Luxury Home Listing

**When To Use:** Properties priced in the top 10% of your market. Homes with premium finishes, architectural significance, or exclusive locations. Any property where the buyer is purchasing a lifestyle, not just square footage.

**Context To Gather:**
- Property address and list price
- Square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms
- 5-7 standout architectural or design features (chef's kitchen, spa bathroom, wine cellar, etc.)
- The "hero feature" -- the single most impressive element of the home
- Neighborhood prestige indicators (gated community, golf course adjacent, waterfront, etc.)
- 2-3 lifestyle details (entertaining spaces, views, privacy)
- Recent upgrades or renovations with approximate values

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a luxury real estate copywriter who writes for Sotheby's International Realty. Your descriptions are evocative but never hyperbolic. You sell lifestyle, not just floor plans.

Write a luxury home listing description using the details below. Follow these rules:
- Open with a single powerful sentence that names the home's defining feature
- Use sensory language sparingly (one or two instances max)
- Include specific, named details (brands, materials, designers) -- never generic "high-end finishes"
- Describe rooms as experiences, not measurements
- Close with a line that makes the reader feel like they have already missed their chance unless they act now
- Length: 300-400 words
- Tone: Confident, understated elegance. Never use exclamation points.

PROPERTY DETAILS:
- Address: [INSERT]
- Price: [INSERT]
- Beds/Baths: [INSERT] / [INSERT]
- Square Footage: [INSERT]
- Key Features: [INSERT 5-7]
- Hero Feature: [INSERT]
- Neighborhood: [INSERT]
- Lifestyle Details: [INSERT]
- Recent Upgrades: [INSERT]
```

### LISTING PROMPT 2: The First-Time Buyer Home

**When To Use:** Starter homes, condos, townhouses, and any property under the median price point in your market. Properties where the emotional driver is possibility, security, and the excitement of first ownership.

**Context To Gather:**
- Property address and list price
- Square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms
- 3-4 features that make this home move-in ready
- 2-3 features that show "room to grow" (unfinished basement, expandable attic, etc.)
- Neighborhood perks for young buyers (parks, coffee shops, commute time)
- School district information
- Any recent updates (new roof, HVAC, appliances)
- First-time buyer program compatibility (FHA, USDA, down payment assistance)

**The Prompt:**

```

Write a listing description for a first-time buyer home. Follow these rules:
- Open by validating the reader's desire to stop renting and start building equity
- Focus on practical value: what they get for their money, what's been updated, what they won't have to fix
- Highlight the monthly payment advantage over renting (if you know it)
- Describe spaces in terms of real-life use (not "breakfast nook" -- say "the spot where you will drink coffee while scrolling your phone")
- Include neighborhood perks that matter to younger buyers
- Mention loan program eligibility naturally (do not lead with it)
- Close with a call to action that feels encouraging, not pushy
- Length: 200-300 words
- Tone: Warm, practical, encouraging. Like a financially-savvy older sibling giving advice.

### LISTING PROMPT 3: The Fixer-Upper

**When To Use:** Properties needing significant cosmetic or structural work. Estate sales, dated homes in great locations, investor specials. Homes where the value proposition is potential, not current condition.

**Context To Gather:**
- Property address and list price
- Square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms, lot size
- The honest condition assessment (what works, what doesn't)
- 3-5 specific opportunities for value-add improvement
- Comparable renovated values in the neighborhood (what it could be worth)
- The "bones" -- what is fundamentally sound (location, structure, layout, lot)
- Any recent inspections or estimates available
- Neighborhood comps to establish ARV (After Repair Value)

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate copywriter who specializes in fixer-upper and investor properties. You do not hide the work needed -- you frame it as an opportunity. Your writing attracts buyers who see potential and understand value. You are honest, direct, and you speak in numbers as much as words.

Write a fixer-upper listing description. Follow these rules:
- Acknowledge the condition upfront -- do not use euphemisms or dance around it
- Immediately pivot to the opportunity: what this property becomes with the right vision
- Use specific dollar ranges or ROI language if appropriate ("homes on this block renovated sell for $X")
- Highlight what is already good: location, lot, layout, structural integrity
- Appeal to two buyer types simultaneously: the investor who sees a flip AND the owner-occupant who wants sweat equity
- Include language about inspections and due diligence
- Close with a challenge: "properties like this reward the buyer who..."
- Length: 200-300 words
- Tone: Honest, direct, data-informed. Respectful of the buyer's intelligence.

PROPERTY DETAILS:
- Address: [INSERT]
- Price: [INSERT]
- Beds/Baths: [INSERT] / [INSERT]
- Square Footage / Lot: [INSERT]
- Condition Assessment: [INSERT honest details]
- Value-Add Opportunities: [INSERT 3-5]
- Renovated Comps: [INSERT ARV range]
- What's Good: [INSERT bones/location/layout]
- Available Reports: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> *For a $175,000 4-bedroom colonial in a neighborhood where renovated homes sell for $400K+*
>
> Let us be direct: 235 Elm Street needs everything. The kitchen is original to 1972. The bathrooms are pink and blue tile that someone thought was stylish when Nixon was in office. The carpets have seen decades of pets and parties. The HVAC is on borrowed time. If you are looking for move-in ready, this is not your house.
>
> But if you are looking for a 2,400-square-foot colonial on a quarter-acre lot in the Westfield school district for under $200,000 -- a neighborhood where the last four renovated comps on this block closed at $415,000, $438,000, $401,000, and $452,000 -- then 235 Elm Street might be the best opportunity in the county.
>
> Here is what is already right: the floor plan is classic center-hall colonial, and it flows. The hardwood floors exist under every square foot of that unfortunate carpet (we checked). The windows were replaced in 2015. The roof has five to seven years left. The foundation is dry and level. The lot is flat, fenced, and gets southern exposure -- the garden potential alone is significant.
>
> The math is straightforward. A gut renovation at $125-150 per square foot puts you all-in around $475,000-535,000. Renovated homes on this block trade between $400,000 and $455,000 depending on finish level. That is not a get-rich flip. That IS a chance to build $60,000-80,000 in sweat equity if you can do some of the work yourself, or a solid buy-and-hold rental at a basis that cash-flows from day one.
>
> We have a pre-listing inspection available and a contractor's scope-of-work estimate for a cosmetic renovation. Bring your contractor, bring your inspector, bring your calculator.
>
> Properties like this reward the buyer who can see past beige Formica to the bones underneath. The bones here are very, very good.

### LISTING PROMPT 7: Price Reduction Announcement

- Seller motivation (relocation, timeline change, already bought next home)
- Days on market or original list date
- 3 strongest features of the property
- Current market context (seasonal slowdown, rate changes, comparable reductions)

```

1. A HEADLINE that announces the change without using the word "reduced" or "dropped"

- Never say "price reduced" in the headline -- use "new price," "repositioned," "updated pricing," etc.

- Include the exact dollar amount of savings to create a tangible before/after comparison
- If days on market is high, do not mention it. If it is low, mention it ("repositioned early to generate multiple offers")
- Create urgency: the new price opens the buyer pool wider
- Tone: Strategic, confident, matter-of-fact. This is an opportunity, not a concession.

## BONUS 1: How to Set Up ChatGPT Custom Instructions for Real Estate

ChatGPT (and Claude, and other AI assistants) allows you to set "custom instructions" -- permanent context that the AI applies to every conversation. This is the single highest-leverage thing you can do to improve AI output quality. Without custom instructions, the AI is guessing who you are and what you need. With them, it becomes your specialized real estate assistant.

### Step-by-Step Setup

**In ChatGPT:**
1. Click your profile icon (bottom left) > Customize ChatGPT
2. Two boxes will appear: "What would you like ChatGPT to know about you?" and "How would you like ChatGPT to respond?"
3. Use the templates below

**In Claude:**
1. Click your profile > Settings > Profile > Set Custom Instructions
2. Use the combined template below

### Custom Instructions Template

Copy and paste the following. Customize the bracketed sections.

**Box 1: What would you like ChatGPT to know about you?**

```
I am a licensed real estate agent in [STATE]. I have been in the business for [X] years and close approximately [X] transactions per year. My market is [CITY/REGION], which is a [describe your market: fast-growing suburban, urban luxury, rural, coastal, etc.] market.

My average price point is $[X]. I work with both buyers and sellers, with a primary focus on [buyers/sellers/both]. My clients are typically [describe: first-time homebuyers, luxury buyers, relocating families, downsizing empty-nesters, investors, etc.].

I write constantly for my business: listing descriptions, client emails, social media content, newsletters, market updates, offer letters, and negotiation communications. I value clear, direct language that sounds like a real person wrote it -- not corporate marketing copy. My communication style is [describe: warm and approachable / polished and professional / direct and data-driven / casual and conversational].

I never use real estate cliches like "dream home," "pride of ownership," or "must-see." I avoid exclamation points in professional writing. I believe good real estate writing is specific, honest, and helpful.
```

**Box 2: How would you like ChatGPT to respond?**

```
When I ask you to write something for my real estate business, follow these rules:

1. Write like a knowledgeable, experienced agent speaking to clients, not a marketing department writing ad copy.
2. Be specific, not generic. Use concrete details, not vague adjectives.
3. Avoid real estate cliches: no "dream home," "pride of ownership," "stunning," "breathtaking," "must-see," "won't last long."
4. Vary sentence length. Mix short declarative sentences with longer explanatory ones.
5. Never use exclamation points unless I specifically ask you to.
6. When writing about a property, describe rooms as experiences, not dimensions.
7. When writing to clients, sound like a trusted advisor, not a salesperson.
8. When writing market content, lead with data, not opinion.
9. Default to a tone that is confident, warm, and direct -- like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about real estate.
10. If I do not provide enough context, ask me for the specific details you need rather than making assumptions.
```

### Why Custom Instructions Matter

Without custom instructions, ChatGPT produces generic real estate content that sounds like it was written by a national brokerage's marketing intern. With custom instructions, it produces content that sounds like YOU. The difference is the difference between content your clients ignore and content they respond to.

## BONUS 2: The "AI Assistant" Voice -- How to Make AI Sound Like You

The biggest complaint agents have about AI content is that it "sounds like AI." Generic. Wordy. Full of adjectives that mean nothing. Here is how to fix that.

### The AI Voice Calibration Exercise

Do this once. It takes 20 minutes and permanently improves every piece of AI-generated content you produce from that point forward.

**Step 1: Gather Your Voice Samples**

Find 3-5 pieces of writing you are proud of. These could be:
- A listing description you wrote that got compliments
- An email to a client that felt genuinely you
- A social media post that got strong engagement
- A market update you wrote yourself

Paste them into a document. These are your voice calibration samples.

**Step 2: Run the Voice Analysis Prompt**

Paste the following prompt into ChatGPT or Claude, along with your voice samples:

```
I am going to share several pieces of my own writing with you. I want you to analyze my writing voice and create a "voice guide" I can use to prompt you to write more like me in the future.

Please analyze:
- My sentence structure (long vs. short sentences, complexity)
- My vocabulary (formal vs. casual, industry jargon level, favorite words or phrases)
- My tone (the emotional quality of my writing -- warm? direct? humorous? serious?)
- My punctuation habits (do I use dashes? parentheticals? short paragraphs?)
- My transition style (how I move from one idea to the next)
- My "signature moves" (things I do repeatedly that define my voice)

Then write a short "Voice Profile" (200 words max) that I can paste at the beginning of any future prompt to get output that sounds like me.

Here are my writing samples:

[PASTE YOUR 3-5 SAMPLES HERE]
```

**Step 3: Save Your Voice Profile**

The AI will output a Voice Profile that looks something like this:

> Voice Profile: Write in short, declarative sentences. Lead with the strongest point. Use concrete, specific language -- no vague adjectives. Paragraphs are short (1-3 sentences). Tone is confident but not arrogant, warm but not sentimental. You use dashes for emphasis -- like this -- and occasional sentence fragments for rhythm. Industry terms are used naturally, not explained. You sound like someone who has done this a long time and genuinely enjoys it.

Save this Voice Profile. Add it to your custom instructions or paste it at the top of any prompt when you want the output to sound authentically like you.

### Quick Fix: The "Kill the Adjectives" Rule

If you do nothing else, do this: after the AI generates content, Ctrl+F for the following words and replace or delete them:

- Stunning
- Breathtaking
- Amazing
- Incredible
- Beautiful (unless followed by a specific detail)
- Gorgeous
- Dream (as in "dream home" or "dream kitchen")
- Pride of ownership
- Must-see
- Won't last long
- This one won't last
- Gem
- Rare find

Replace each one with a specific, concrete detail. Instead of "stunning kitchen," write "kitchen with a 36-inch gas range, quartz counters, and a window over the sink that faces the garden." Specificity sells. Adjectives fill space.

## BONUS 3: Automating with Zapier + ChatGPT

This section shows you how to connect ChatGPT to the tools you already use, so AI output flows automatically into your workflows instead of you copying and pasting all day.

### Conceptual Overview

The basic automation pattern works like this:

1. A trigger event happens (new lead in your CRM, new email from a client, new listing in your MLS)
2. Zapier detects the trigger and sends the data to ChatGPT with a prompt
3. ChatGPT generates a response (a follow-up email, a listing description draft, a market update)
4. Zapier sends the response to your destination (your email, your CRM, a Google Doc, your task manager)

### Automation Idea 1: Auto-Draft Listing Descriptions

- **Trigger:** New listing added to your CRM or a Google Sheet
- **Action:** ChatGPT generates a listing description using Prompt #1-15 from this guide
- **Output:** Draft is saved to a Google Doc, ready for your review and personalization
- **Time saved:** 30-45 minutes per listing

### Automation Idea 2: Instant Lead Follow-Up

- **Trigger:** New lead captured from your website, Zillow, or realtor.com
- **Action:** ChatGPT generates a personalized follow-up email (using Communication Prompt #1)
- **Output:** Email is drafted in your Gmail or sent to your CRM as a task
- **Time saved:** 10-15 minutes per lead

### Automation Idea 3: Weekly Market Update Newsletter

- **Trigger:** Scheduled every Monday at 8 AM
- **Action:** Zapier pulls MLS data (or you input it manually), sends to ChatGPT with Market Update Prompt (#10 in Communication)
- **Output:** Complete newsletter draft saved to Google Docs
- **Time saved:** 1-2 hours per week

### Automation Idea 4: Social Media Content Calendar

- **Trigger:** Scheduled every Sunday at 5 PM
- **Action:** ChatGPT generates 7 social media posts for the week (using Social Media Prompts #1-15)
- **Output:** Posts saved to a Google Sheet with scheduled publish dates
- **Time saved:** 2-3 hours per week

### Getting Started with Zapier

1. Create a free Zapier account (zapier.com)
2. Connect your apps (Gmail, Google Sheets, CRM, etc.)
3. Create a "Zap" -- a trigger + action workflow
4. In the action step, choose "ChatGPT" as the app
5. Paste your prompt (from this guide) and map the fields (Zapier will pull data from your trigger)
6. Test the Zap with real data
7. Turn it on

**Note on security:** Never send sensitive client information (SSNs, full financial details, legal documents) through automation tools. Use these automations for marketing content, drafts, and internal workflows only. Client-confidential information stays in your secure systems.

## BONUS 4: Connecting AI to Your MLS Data (Conceptual)

This section is conceptual -- it explains what is possible and how to think about it. Implementation will depend on your MLS, your technical resources, and your comfort level. This is the direction the industry is heading. Agents who understand this now will be ahead of the curve.

### What "Connecting AI to MLS" Means

Your MLS contains the most valuable real estate data in your market: sold prices, days on market, price per square foot, inventory levels, price reductions, listing history. Right now, you access this data manually -- you pull comps, you run reports, you export spreadsheets.

The concept: connect your MLS data to an AI tool so the AI can analyze it directly and produce insights, narratives, and recommendations instantly, without you manually feeding it data each time.

### Three Levels of Integration

**Level 1: Manual (You are here)**
You pull MLS data, copy it, paste it into ChatGPT, and ask for analysis. This is what every prompt in this guide assumes. It works. It takes 5-10 minutes per use.

**Level 2: Semi-Automated (Achievable now)**
You set up MLS auto-emails or data exports to send you weekly/monthly reports. You forward those emails to a Zapier-connected ChatGPT that generates a market narrative or client-ready summary automatically. You review before sending. This is achievable with current tools and takes an afternoon to set up.

**Level 3: API Integration (Future state)**
Your MLS provides an API (application programming interface) that allows software to pull data directly. You connect that API to an AI tool that queries the MLS in real time -- "Show me every 3-bedroom home sold in the last 90 days within a half-mile of 312 Maple, and tell me the price trend." This is the future. A few tech-forward MLSs offer this today. Most do not. But they will.

### What You Can Do Today

Even without full MLS integration, here is what is possible with the tools you already have:

1. **Automated CMA Narratives:** Export your CMA spreadsheet as a CSV. Upload it to ChatGPT with Business Prompt #1. Get a complete narrative in 30 seconds.

2. **Market Trend Reports:** Export your MLS market statistics report. Upload it. Get a client-ready newsletter in 60 seconds (Communication Prompt #10).

3. **Neighborhood Price Analysis:** Pull the last 12 months of closed sales for a neighborhood. Upload the data. Ask: "What is the price trend, what is the average days on market, and what does this mean for a seller listing now?"

4. **Buyer Match Reports:** Export active listings that match a buyer's criteria. Ask the AI to rank them by value (price per square foot vs. neighborhood average) and write a one-sentence note about each one.

Most agents use AI to write listing descriptions. That is table stakes now. The agents who will dominate in five years are the ones who use AI to analyze market data and generate insights that other agents cannot replicate without hours of manual work.
