### BUSINESS PROMPT 1: CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) Narrative

**When To Use:** Writing the narrative summary that accompanies a Comparative Market Analysis. The numbers are in the spreadsheet -- this is the story that explains what the numbers mean and what the seller should do.

**Context To Gather:**
- Property address
- Your recommended list price range
- 3-5 comparable sold properties (address, sale date, price, key similarities/differences)
- 2-3 comparable active listings (competition)
- Average days on market for the area
- Market trends (prices rising/flat/falling, inventory up/down)
- Seasonality considerations

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate market analyst writing the narrative summary for a Comparative Market Analysis. The spreadsheet has all the numbers -- your job is to translate those numbers into a clear, confident pricing recommendation that the seller can understand and trust.

Write a CMA narrative summary. Follow these rules:
- Present the recommended price range in the first paragraph and own it -- no hedging
- Summarize the key comps in narrative form (not a list): what they sold for, how they compare, why your seller's home is positioned where it is
- Address the active competition: who else is on the market, what they are asking, and why your seller's home will win at the recommended price
- Include market trend context: what direction the market is moving and what that means for pricing strategy
- End with a clear recommendation and the rationale behind choosing this specific price point
- Length: 350-500 words
- Tone: Authoritative, data-driven, clear. You are the expert and you have the receipts.

CMA DETAILS:
- Address: [INSERT]
- Recommended Price Range: [INSERT]
- Sold Comps: [INSERT 3-5 with key details]
- Active Comps: [INSERT 2-3]
- Avg DOM: [INSERT]
- Market Trends: [INSERT]
- Seasonality: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> **CMA Narrative: 312 Brookstone Drive**
>
> **Recommended List Price: $575,000 - $585,000**
>
> This recommendation is based on an analysis of five closed sales in Brookstone Estates over the last 90 days, three active listings currently competing for the same buyer, and current market trajectory.
>
> **The Market Context**
>
> Brookstone homes in the $550K-$625K range are selling in an average of 18 days, with a list-to-close-price ratio of 98.4%. Inventory in this price band is 2.3 months of supply -- still a seller's market, but not the frenzy of 2024-2025. Buyers have enough options to be selective. They are not overpaying for homes that show poorly or are priced aspirationally.
>
> **The Sold Comparables**
>
> Five closed sales establish the value range for 312 Brookstone Drive:
>
> - 2411 Brookstone (closed March 12): $579,000. Same floor plan as 312 Brookstone. Similar lot size. Comparable condition, though 312 Brookstone has a renovated kitchen (2022) that 2411 Brookstone lacked. This is the strongest direct comp and supports a price at or above $579,000.
>
> - 2405 Brookstone (closed February 28 ): $592,000. Larger floor plan (+400 SF), finished basement, pool. Superior to 312 Brookstone on square footage and amenities. This sale represents the ceiling of the range -- 312 Brookstone should not exceed this number.
>
> - 2430 Brookstone (closed March 25): $568,000. Same floor plan. No kitchen renovation. Sold with dated bathrooms and original HVAC. Inferior to 312 Brookstone on condition. This sale supports the floor of the range.
>
> - 3108 Brookstone (closed January 15): $583,000. Comparable size, similar updates. This sale had a finished basement (312 does not) but an inferior lot (backed to the community entrance). The net comparison is neutral.
>
> - 2418 Brookstone (closed February 5): $571,000. Smaller floor plan. Inferior condition. Sold as-is. An outlier that demonstrates what happens when a home is not market-ready -- we mention it because your buyer may mention it, but it is not a pricing factor for 312 Brookstone.
>
> **The Active Competition**
>
> Three homes are actively listed in Brookstone in the $550K-$625K range:
>
> - 2501 Brookstone: Listed at $599,000. Larger home, finished basement, but original 2005 kitchen and baths. At this price, buyers expect updates that this home does not have. It has been on the market 34 days -- validating that the market is rejecting the price.
>
> - 2435 Brookstone: Listed at $579,000. Same floor plan as 312. No significant updates in 10 years. Shows adequately. This is your most direct competition at the recommended price level.
>
> - 2510 Brookstone: Listed at $619,000. New construction from a custom builder. Different buyer entirely -- the buyer who wants new and will pay for it. Not a direct comp but worth mentioning because it anchors the high end of the range.
>
> **Pricing Strategy**
>
> At $575,000-$585,000, 312 Brookstone enters the market as the best value among the active competition. The renovated kitchen differentiates it from 2435 Brookstone (listed at the same price without updates). The price leaves room for the market to bid up -- and in a market where homes are selling at 98.4% of asking in 18 days, we want to attract multiple offers in the first week rather than price high and negotiate down over 30-45 days.
>
> If the market supports it, offers will push the final sale price toward $590,000-$595,000. If we price at $599,000 out of the gate, we sit alongside 2501 Brookstone -- the house with 34 days on market and no offers.
>
> I recommend listing at $579,000 to maximize first-week traffic and position for multiple offers.

### BUSINESS PROMPT 2: Weekly Business Plan

**When To Use:** Creating a structured weekly plan that balances lead generation, active client service, transaction management, and personal development. This is your operating system for the week.

**Context To Gather:**
- Number of active buyers and sellers
- Number of pending transactions
- Lead generation goals for the week (calls, contacts, appointments)
- Specific follow-ups due
- Any upcoming deadlines (closings, inspection periods, option periods)
- Personal or professional development goal for the week
- Any time blocked for non-business activities

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate business coach helping an agent structure their most productive week. You understand that a real estate agent's week is divided into four buckets: lead generation, active client service, transaction management, and business development. A good week balances all four.

Write a weekly business plan template that the agent fills in with their specific numbers and activities. Include:

1. TOP 3 PRIORITIES (the three things that, if nothing else gets done, make this week a win)
2. LEAD GENERATION (specific activities and targets)
3. ACTIVE CLIENT SERVICE (buyers, sellers, key actions for each)
4. TRANSACTION MANAGEMENT (pending deals, critical deadlines, checklist items)
5. BUSINESS DEVELOPMENT (skills, systems, networking, learning)
6. WEEKLY METRICS TRACKER (calls made, contacts added, appointments set, etc.)
7. END-OF-WEEK REFLECTION (what worked, what didn't, what to carry forward)

Rules:
- Make it specific enough to be actionable, flexible enough to be usable every week
- Include estimated time blocks for each category
- Build in accountability: numbers to track, results to measure
- Length: Structure should fill 1-2 pages when printed
- Tone: Direct, motivating, practical. This is your operating system, not your inspiration board.

WEEKLY DETAILS:
- Active Buyers: [INSERT number]
- Active Sellers: [INSERT number]
- Pending Transactions: [INSERT number]
- Lead Gen Goal: [INSERT calls, contacts, appointments]
- Follow-Ups Due: [INSERT]
- Deadlines: [INSERT]
- Development Goal: [INSERT]
```

### BUSINESS PROMPT 3: Lead Follow-Up Drip Sequence

**When To Use:** Creating a multi-touch follow-up sequence for different lead types (online leads, open house leads, past client referrals, etc.). This is the email/text sequence that converts leads into appointments.

**Context To Gather:**
- Lead source (Zillow, realtor.com, open house, referral, past client, social media, etc.)
- Lead temperature (hot = actively looking, warm = 3-6 months out, cold = just browsing)
- Number of touches in the sequence (3-touch, 5-touch, 7-touch)
- Channel mix (email, text, phone, video message)
- Time intervals between touches
- The desired outcome (schedule a call, set a showing, come to an open house, etc.)

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate lead conversion specialist designing a follow-up sequence for [INSERT lead type and temperature] leads. Your sequence moves leads from "who are you" to "let's meet" through strategic, value-driven touches that feel helpful, not desperate.

Design a [INSERT number]-touch follow-up sequence. For each touch, provide:

1. Timing (when it sends relative to the trigger event)
2. Channel (email, text, phone, video message)
3. Content summary (what this touch says or provides)
4. CTA (what you want the lead to do)
5. Subject line or text opener (the exact first words they see)

Rules:
- Touch 1 is always immediate (within minutes or hours) and provides value, not a pitch
- Every touch must include something useful: a tip, a market stat, a relevant listing, an answer to a common question
- The "ask" escalates gradually: from "open this" to "reply to this" to "let's meet"
- Mix channels: do not send 5 emails in a row. Alternate email, text, call, video.
- Include one "breakup" touch at the end that gracefully closes the loop
- Tone: Helpful, persistent without being annoying, confident without being pushy.

LEAD DETAILS:
- Lead Source: [INSERT]
- Lead Temperature: [INSERT hot/warm/cold]
- Number of Touches: [INSERT]
- Desired Outcome: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> **5-Touch Follow-Up Sequence: Online Lead (Zillow/realtor.com inquiry), Warm Temperature**
>
> ---
>
> **TOUCH 1: Immediate Response Email**
> **Timing:** Within 15 minutes of inquiry
> **Channel:** Email
>
> Subject: Thanks for reaching out about [Property Address] -- one thing I want you to know
>
> Content: Thank them for their inquiry. Confirm the property is available (or update them on status). Provide ONE piece of information they would not get from the Zillow listing (property history, neighborhood insight, recent price changes, seller motivation if known). Ask one qualifying question: "Are you working with an agent already? (Totally fine if so -- just want to make sure I am not stepping on anyone's toes.)"
>
> CTA: Reply with your answer to the agent question and let me know if you want to see this home in person.
>
> ---
>
> **TOUCH 2: Value Text (48 hours later)**
> **Timing:** 2 days after Touch 1 (send at 10 AM)
> **Channel:** Text message
>
> Text: "Hi [Name], it's [Agent Name]. Quick thing I thought you'd want to see -- [Property Address] has a showing scheduled tomorrow and an open house this Saturday 1-3 PM. If you are interested, I would try to see it before the open house when it is quieter and you can take your time. Let me know if you want me to set that up."
>
> CTA: Text back yes/no/not interested.
>
> ---
>
> **TOUCH 3: Market Insight Email (Day 5)**
> **Timing:** 5 days after Touch 1
> **Channel:** Email
>
> Subject: Three other homes like [original property] (but different)
>
> Content: Acknowledge that the original property may or may not be the right fit. Present 2-3 similar properties in their price range/area -- one that is better value, one that is more updated, one that is in a slightly different neighborhood. For each, give one sentence on why it might work for them. Ask: "Did any of these catch your eye?"
>
> CTA: Reply and tell me which one (if any) you want to learn more about. Or tell me what was off about the ones I sent -- that helps me narrow it down.
>
> ---
>
> **TOUCH 4: Personal Video Message (Day 8-10)**
> **Timing:** 8-10 days after Touch 1
> **Channel:** Video text (BombBomb, Loom, or recorded on phone)
>
> Content: 60-second video. "Hey [Name], [Agent Name] here. I know you reached out about [property] a little over a week ago and I wanted to put a face with the name. Quick market update: [1 short, relevant stat]. Also, I have a new listing coming up in [area] that is not on Zillow yet -- [brief description]. If you are still looking, I would love to help. No pressure at all. If your plans changed, totally get it. Just shoot me a text either way. Thanks!"
>
> CTA: Text me to set up a call, or text me if your plans changed (no hard feelings).
>
> ---
>
> **TOUCH 5: Breakup Email (Day 14)**
> **Timing:** 14 days after Touch 1
> **Channel:** Email
>
> Subject: Closing the loop -- one last thing
>
> Content: "Hi [Name] -- I have reached out a few times since you inquired about [property] and I have not heard back. No worries at all -- I know plans change and sometimes the timing is not right. I am going to close the loop on my end so I am not cluttering your inbox. If you ever want to restart the search -- next month, next year, whenever -- just text or email me. I will pick it right back up. In the meantime, if you want to keep an eye on the market passively, I send a monthly update with local stats and new listings. Zero pressure, zero follow-up calls. Let me know if you want to be on that list. Either way, I hope you find the right home when the time is right."
>
> CTA: Reply "monthly" if you want the market update newsletter. Otherwise, no response needed -- I will leave you alone.
