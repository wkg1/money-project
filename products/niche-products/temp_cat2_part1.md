### COMMUNICATION PROMPT 1: Buyer Consultation Follow-Up

**When To Use:** After an initial buyer consultation where you discussed their needs, budget, timeline, and the buying process. This email reinforces your value and moves them toward the next step.

**Context To Gather:**
- Buyer name(s) and any personal details discussed
- Their target price range, preferred neighborhoods, and must-haves
- Timeline for purchase
- Pre-approval status (and lender contact if known)
- 1-2 specific things you discussed that show you were listening
- Next step you want them to take

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate agent writing a follow-up email to a buyer you just met for the first time. Your goal is to reinforce their confidence in you, summarize what you discussed so they feel heard, and move them to the next concrete step.

Write the email. Follow these rules:
- Reference at least one personal detail they shared that has nothing to do with real estate (their dog, their job, their kids, their hobby)
- Summarize their search criteria back to them in a way that shows you understood the WHY, not just the WHAT
- Include one piece of market insight they probably did not know before meeting you
- Give them one clear, easy next step with a specific deadline or timeframe
- Make it feel personal, not templated. If someone forwarded this email to a colleague, it would be obvious it was written specifically for this buyer
- Length: 200-250 words
- Tone: Warm, competent, low-pressure. Like a trusted advisor, not a salesperson.

BUYER DETAILS:
- Name(s): [INSERT]
- Personal Detail: [INSERT something they shared about their life]
- Price Range: [INSERT]
- Preferred Areas: [INSERT]
- Must-Haves: [INSERT]
- Timeline: [INSERT]
- Pre-Approval Status: [INSERT]
- Market Insight To Share: [INSERT one thing they didn't know]
- Next Step: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> *For a couple looking for their first home with a baby on the way:*
>
> Subject: Great meeting you both -- next steps and one thing I forgot to mention
>
> Hi Sarah and Marcus,
>
> I really enjoyed meeting you both yesterday. You are asking all the right questions for first-time buyers, and the fact that you have already talked to a lender puts you ahead of 90% of the buyers I meet.
>
> I have been thinking about what you said about wanting a neighborhood where you can walk to things. With the baby coming (congratulations again -- November babies are the best, my niece is a November baby and she is the coolest kid I know), I think you are going to care about walkability even more than you realize. Being able to stroll to a coffee shop or a park instead of buckling a car seat every single time is a quality-of-life upgrade you cannot put a price on.
>
> Here is a quick recap of what we are looking for: 3+ bedrooms, 2+ baths, under $425,000, with a preference for Oak Park, Riverside, or the north side of Westdale. Good light, some character, a kitchen you can actually cook in, and at least a small yard for future little-leaguer Marcus Jr. Move-in ready preferred (you have enough projects coming in November).
>
> One thing I forgot to mention when we met: there is a pocket of homes in the Oak Park Elementary district that never hit the MLS because they trade agent-to-agent before going public. I want to make sure you see those. That means getting you set up with a search in the MLS, but also doing some direct outreach to agents who have upcoming listings in those neighborhoods.
>
> First step: if you have not already, send over your pre-approval letter when it is ready. No rush, but having that in hand means we can move instantly when the right house appears -- and in this market, the right house rewards the quick.
>
> I will set up your MLS search this afternoon and you will start seeing matches by tonight. Text me if anything jumps out and we will get you in the door.
>
> Talk soon,
> [Your Name]

### COMMUNICATION PROMPT 2: Seller Market Analysis Presentation

**When To Use:** When presenting a CMA (Comparative Market Analysis) to a potential seller. This is often the first formal communication where you demonstrate your value and market knowledge.

**Context To Gather:**
- Seller name(s) and property address
- Your recommended list price range and justification
- 3 comparable sold properties (with addresses, dates, prices)
- 2-3 comparable active listings (competition)
- Average days on market for the area
- Your marketing plan highlights (2-3 specific differentiators)
- Any prep/staging recommendations before listing
- Your commission structure and what it includes

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a listing agent preparing the written component of a CMA presentation to a homeowner. This is not the full CMA (the numbers will be in a separate document or slide deck). This is the cover email or executive summary that frames the numbers and positions you as the right agent for the job.

Write the seller CMA cover communication. Follow these rules:
- Lead by acknowledging this is a big decision and you respect that
- Present the price recommendation with confidence, supported by a brief data point (do not dump all the comps here)
- Address the competition honestly -- who else is on the market and why your seller's home will stand out
- Mention 2-3 specific marketing tactics that differentiate you from other agents
- Include estimated timeline from list to close
- Invite questions and make it easy for them to say yes
- Length: 250-350 words
- Tone: Confident expertise. You are the authority, but you are also on their side.

SELLER DETAILS:
- Names: [INSERT]
- Address: [INSERT]
- Recommended Price: [INSERT range]
- Key Comp (Sold): [INSERT 1 strongest]
- Competition (Active): [INSERT 2-3]
- Avg DOM: [INSERT]
- Marketing Highlights: [INSERT 2-3]
- Prep Recommendations: [INSERT]
- Estimated Timeline: [INSERT]
- Commission/Inclusions: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> Subject: 2410 Hawthorne Lane -- Market Analysis and Recommended Strategy
>
> Hi David and Lisa,
>
> Thank you for inviting me into your home yesterday. You have maintained it beautifully -- that kitchen alone is going to do a lot of the selling for us.
>
> I have completed the full CMA, which is attached. Here is the executive summary and my recommendation.
>
> After analyzing the last six months of comparable sales in Hawthorne Estates and the three adjacent subdivisions, I am recommending a list price of $525,000 to $539,000. The most relevant comp supporting this: 2418 Hawthorne (your same floor plan, one street over) closed at $532,000 in March with fewer updates and a smaller lot. Your home compares favorably on every variable.
>
> The market window is open but specific. There are currently four homes active in the $500K-$550K range in our target area, averaging 28 days on market. Three of those four need significant cosmetic work. Your home, because of the kitchen renovation, the hardwoods throughout, and the landscaping, enters the market at the top of its class. I want to price it to sell in the first two weeks, while we have the "new listing" attention and before any of those competing homes drop their prices.
>
> A few things I will do that most agents in our market do not:
>
> 1. Professional photography, drone shots, and a 90-second walkthrough video are included. I use a real architectural photographer, not a wide-angle iPhone.
> 2. I run targeted social ads to a custom audience of people who have searched for homes in this price range in our zip code in the last 90 days. Average ad spend is $500, and I cover it.
> 3. I host a broker's open with lunch on the Tuesday before the public open house. Getting 30 agents through the door before the weekend creates word-of-mouth that no Zillow listing can replicate.
>
> Timeline: with your go-ahead, we can have photos shot this week, the listing live by next Tuesday, and open houses the following weekend. From list to close, I would estimate 45-60 days assuming a standard contract.
>
> Before we go live, I have a short punch list of prep items -- nothing major, just the small things that photograph well and signal "well-maintained" to buyers. I will send that separately.
>
> I would love to earn your business. If the strategy makes sense, let me know and I will send over the listing agreement for your review. I am also happy to walk through any of the comps or numbers in more detail -- whatever helps you feel confident.
>
> Talk soon,
> [Your Name]

### COMMUNICATION PROMPT 3: Offer Presentation Letter

**When To Use:** When presenting an offer to a seller (as a listing agent) or when your buyer is submitting an offer and you want to include a cover letter that frames the offer positively.

**Context To Gather:**
- Offer price and terms
- Financing type and down payment
- Contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal, etc.)
- Closing timeline
- Any non-monetary benefits of this buyer (flexible close, rent-back offered, all-cash, etc.)
- Buyer's story (why they want the home -- emotional context)
- How the offer compares to list price (above, at, below)
- Any competing offers to disclose

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a buyer's agent writing a cover letter to accompany an offer on a home. Your job is to humanize the numbers, frame the offer's strengths, and address potential concerns before the listing agent raises them.

Write an offer cover letter. Follow these rules:
- Open by stating the offer exists and one sentence about who the buyer is (humanize them)
- Present the strongest terms FIRST (do not bury the lead -- if it is above asking or all-cash, lead with it)
- If the offer is below asking, address it directly with market-based justification, not apology
- Frame contingencies not as demands but as the buyer being prudent and prepared
- Close by making it easy for the seller to say yes (emphasize closing certainty, flexibility, or speed)
- Length: 200-300 words
- Tone: Professional, respectful, confident. You are advocating for your buyer, not begging.

OFFER DETAILS:
- Offer Price: [INSERT]
- Financing: [INSERT]
- Down Payment: [INSERT]
- Contingencies: [INSERT]
- Closing Timeline: [INSERT]
- Buyer Strengths: [INSERT non-monetary or structural advantages]
- Buyer Story: [INSERT brief]
- vs List Price: [INSERT above/at/below]
- Competing Offers: [INSERT if known]
```

**Example Output:**

> Subject: Offer on 1842 Ridgeway Drive -- Pre-Approved, Flexible Close, Love Letter Attached
>
> Hi Jennifer,
>
> Attached please find an offer from my buyers, Tom and Rachel Chen, on 1842 Ridgeway Drive. Tom is a pediatrician at Children's Hospital. Rachel runs a small architecture firm. They have been looking for a mid-century home for eight months and 1842 Ridgeway is the first one that made Rachel -- who is very hard to impress -- say nothing for thirty seconds and then whisper, "This is it."
>
> The offer: $525,000, which is full asking price. Conventional financing with 25% down and a full pre-approval from Wells Fargo (letter attached -- fully underwritten, not just pre-qualified, which means the financing contingency window can be shortened to 14 days). They are offering a 30-day close with the option to extend to 45 days at no penalty if the sellers need more time, because Tom and Rachel's lease runs through August and they are flexible.
>
> The inspection contingency is standard -- 10 days for a full inspection. Tom and Rachel are not looking for a perfect house (Rachel's words: "mid-century homes are supposed to have quirks"). They will not nickel-and-dime on inspection items. The appraisal contingency is included as a formality given the strong comps and their 25% down payment -- if there is an appraisal gap, they have the cash to cover it.
>
> The "love letter" is the last page of the offer packet. Tom and Rachel wrote it themselves. It talks about how they want to raise their kids in a house with history, they name specific original features they noticed, and they promise to be good stewards of a home the sellers clearly loved for 22 years.
>
> Tom and Rachel are move-forward buyers. Pre-approved, motivated, and they do not want to lose this house. I am available to discuss the offer or answer any questions -- call or text anytime.
>
> Thank you,
> [Your Name]

### COMMUNICATION PROMPT 4: Counter-Offer Negotiation Email

**When To Use:** When presenting a counter-offer, either on behalf of a seller responding to a buyer's offer, or on behalf of a buyer making a counter to a seller's counter. This email needs to maintain goodwill while moving the negotiation forward.

**Context To Gather:**
- Original offer terms and counter-offer terms (with differences highlighted)
- The "why" behind the counter (the rationale, not just the numbers)
- Any non-monetary movement (timeline, inclusions, repairs, etc.)
- Comparable data supporting the counter position
- A concession you made (show movement)
- Deadline for response

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate agent writing a counter-offer communication. Your goal is to present the counter clearly, justify the position with data or logic (not emotion), demonstrate that you have already moved toward the other side, and keep the deal alive.

Write a counter-offer communication. Follow these rules:
- Thank the other party for their offer -- genuinely, not perfunctorily
- Present the counter clearly, with the change from the previous offer stated explicitly
- Provide market-based or logical justification for your position (never "my client feels...")
- Highlight at least one area where your side conceded or moved
- Make it easy to continue negotiating -- invite conversation, not ultimatums
- If there is a deadline, state it with a reason, not a threat
- Length: 200-300 words
- Tone: Collaborative, reasonable, data-informed. A negotiation partner, not an adversary.

NEGOTIATION DETAILS:
- Original Offer: [INSERT key terms]
- Counter Offer: [INSERT key terms]
- Rationale For Counter: [INSERT data/logic]
- Your Concession: [INSERT area where you moved]
- Deadline: [INSERT]
- Other Party Context: [INSERT anything known about the other side's position]
```

**Example Output:**

> Subject: Counter-Offer on 1842 Ridgeway Drive -- Movement On Both Sides
>
> Hi Mike,
>
> Thank you and your clients for a thoughtful initial offer on 1842 Ridgeway. I can tell they have done their homework and they genuinely want the home -- that always makes a negotiation better on both sides.
>
> My sellers have reviewed the offer and asked me to present the following counter:
>
> Price: $520,000 (a $5,000 reduction from the $525,000 list price -- the sellers acknowledge the inspection items your buyers flagged, specifically the electrical panel and the water heater age, and have priced those in)
>
> Closing: 30 days, as offered. The sellers appreciate the flexibility your buyers extended and will not need the extra time.
>
> Inspection items: The sellers will credit $3,500 at closing for the electrical panel replacement (the quote from your inspector's report) and will service the HVAC prior to close (receipt provided). The water heater, at seven years old, is functional and was not identified as deficient in the inspection -- the sellers will not replace it but have factored its age into the $5,000 price reduction above.
>
> The sellers also wanted me to mention: the roof warranty transfers to the new owner and has eight years remaining. They are leaving the custom window treatments throughout the house, the Ring doorbell system, and the garage shelving.
>
> The sellers have moved on price, closing costs, and the electrical credit. They feel this counter reflects the inspection findings fairly while keeping the deal at a number that works for everyone. The counter is open until 5 PM Friday -- the sellers would like to have the home under contract before the weekend.
>
> Give me a call if you want to talk through any of the numbers. I think we are 90% of the way to a deal here and I would love to get this across the finish line.
>
> [Your Name]

### COMMUNICATION PROMPT 5: Post-Closing Thank You

**When To Use:** After a successful closing, sent within 24-48 hours. This is a relationship-building touch point that sets up future referrals and repeat business.

**Context To Gather:**
- Client name(s) and property address
- A specific memory from the transaction (something personal or funny)
- A closing gift detail (if you gave one)
- 1-2 local recommendations (restaurant, handyman, etc.) related to their new home
- A referral prompt (gentle, not aggressive)
- Move-in or logistics reminder

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate agent writing a post-closing message to clients. This is not a transactional "thanks for the business" email. This is the start of a long-term relationship. You want them to remember you fondly and call you again in five years.

Write a post-closing message. Follow these rules:
- Reference a specific shared experience from the transaction (shows you were present, not just processing paperwork)
- Celebrate their win genuinely -- this is a big life moment
- Include 1-2 genuinely useful recommendations for their new home/neighborhood
- Mention the referral prompt naturally, not as a separate pitch
- Remind them you exist for future needs without being salesy
- Length: 150-200 words
- Tone: Warm, personal, celebratory. Like a friend who also happens to be really good at real estate.

CLIENT DETAILS:
- Name(s): [INSERT]
- Property Address: [INSERT]
- Shared Memory: [INSERT something specific]
- Closing Gift: [INSERT if applicable]
- Local Recommendations: [INSERT 1-2]
- Referral Prompt: [INSERT soft ask]
```
