### SOCIAL PROMPT 2: TikTok/Reel Script Hook Ideas

**When To Use:** Generating short-form video script ideas for TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts. The hook is everything in short-form video.

**Context To Gather:**
- Video topic (property tour, market tip, agent humor, client story, etc.)
- Target audience (buyers, sellers, first-timers, luxury, etc.)
- Video length (15-60 seconds)
- Your on-camera style (educational, funny, direct, high-energy)
- The one takeaway you want viewers to remember

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a short-form video scriptwriter for a real estate agent. You write TikTok and Reel scripts that hook viewers in the first 1.5 seconds and hold their attention to the end. You understand that short-form real estate content competes with dance videos and comedy sketches -- your scripts must earn every second of attention.

Write 5 hook + script outline options for a short-form real estate video. For each one, provide:
1. The hook (first sentence spoken, or text overlay if using text hook)
2. The 2-3 key points covered in the middle
3. The closing line / CTA
4. Suggested text overlay or caption

Rules:
- Hooks must be under 5 seconds when spoken
- Each option should have a different creative angle (educational, controversial, funny, behind-the-scenes, emotional)
- Scripts feel natural when spoken aloud -- short sentences, conversational rhythm
- Every script has ONE clear idea. Not three ideas fighting for attention.
- No cheesy transitions, no "wait till the end," no "you won't believe"

VIDEO PARAMETERS:
- Topic: [INSERT]
- Target Audience: [INSERT]
- Video Length: [INSERT seconds]
- Style/Energy: [INSERT]
- Key Takeaway: [INSERT]
```

> *For an article about what actually matters when pricing a home:*
>
> **Headline: Your Zestimate Is Not a Valuation. Here Is What Actually Determines Your Home's Price.**
>
> I had a seller last month who was convinced her home was worth $575,000. Her evidence: the Zestimate said $582,000, and her neighbor "knew a guy" who sold for "somewhere around that."
>
> Her home closed last week at $512,000. Not because the market is soft. Because Zestimates do not walk through your kitchen. Because the neighbor's guy sold in 2022 when rates were 3%. Because pricing a home is not a Google search -- it is a forensic exercise in micro-market analysis.
>
> Here is what actually determines your home's market value, in order of importance:
>
> **1. Closed Comps, Not Active Listings**
> What someone else is asking for their home is not what your home is worth. The only numbers that matter are closed sales -- what buyers actually paid -- within the last 90 days, within a half-mile radius, with comparable square footage, bed/bath count, and condition. Everything else is noise. In my market, I use a minimum of five closed comps and weight them by recency and similarity. One outlier comp (the house that sold for way more or way less) gets excluded with an explanation of why.
>
> **2. Condition, Not Square Footage**
> Two homes with identical floor plans can sell $60,000 apart based on condition alone. The market does not pay for potential -- it pays for what it can see, touch, and compare. The kitchen with the 2008 granite and the oak cabinets is not worth the same as the one with the 2022 quartz and flat-panel cabinetry, even if they are the same size. An accurate CMA accounts for condition with adjustment values that reflect what buyers in your specific market actually pay for updates.
>
> **3. Market Velocity, Not National Headlines**
> The national news tells you what mortgage rates did this week. It does not tell you that homes in your specific neighborhood are selling in 9 days with 3 offers on average. Micro-market velocity -- how fast homes are moving at what percentage of asking, in your specific price band and zip code -- is a better predictor of your sale price than any national indicator. I track this weekly for every price band in every neighborhood I serve.
>
> A good agent does not guess at your home's value. They build a data set, apply adjustments, test it against active competition, and present a range with confidence. If your agent cannot show you the five closed sales that support their price recommendation, find a different agent.
>
