### BUSINESS PROMPT 4: Team Meeting Agenda

**When To Use:** Structuring a productive weekly team meeting. This agenda keeps the meeting focused, time-bound, and valuable for everyone attending.

**Context To Gather:**
- Team size and roles
- Meeting duration (30 min, 60 min)
- Key metrics to review (listings, pendings, closings, leads, etc.)
- Any specific wins to celebrate
- Key challenges or blockers to address
- Action items from last meeting

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate team leader designing a weekly team meeting agenda. The agenda must be efficient, accountability-driven, and energizing -- not a time-waster that people dread attending.

Design a team meeting agenda with these components:

1. MEETING DETAILS (duration, day/time, location/virtual link)
2. PRE-MEETING PREP (what each person must bring or update before the meeting)
3. AGENDA SECTIONS with time allocations
4. STANDING AGENDA ITEMS (metrics, deals, training, wins)
5. GROUND RULES for keeping the meeting productive

Rules:
- Total meeting duration should be respected (do not create a 90-minute agenda for a 60-minute meeting)
- Every agenda item answers: who leads it, how long, what decision or outcome are we driving toward
- Build in accountability: last week's commitments should be reviewed before new business
- Include a "wins" section early in the meeting (sets a positive tone)
- End with clear action items, owners, and deadlines
- Length: Agenda should fit on one page
- Tone: Efficient, positive, accountability-focused. This is the operating rhythm of a high-performing team.

TEAM DETAILS:
- Team Size: [INSERT]
- Meeting Duration: [INSERT]
- Meeting Day/Time: [INSERT]
- Key Metrics To Track: [INSERT]
- Recent Wins: [INSERT]
- Challenges To Address: [INSERT]
```

**Example Output:**

> **WEEKLY TEAM HUDDLE AGENDA**
> **Meeting:** Mondays, 9:00-10:00 AM | Conference Room + Zoom
>
> ---
>
> **PRE-MEETING PREP (Complete by Friday 5 PM)**
> - Update your pipeline in the CRM (listings, pendings, active buyers, active sellers)
> - Enter your weekly metrics (calls, appointments, contracts, closings)
> - Add your top 3 priorities for next week
> - Flag any deals that need team problem-solving (red/yellow/green status)
>
> ---
>
> **AGENDA (60 minutes)**
>
> **9:00-9:05 | WINS OF THE WEEK (All, round-robin)**
> - Each person shares ONE win from last week (30 seconds each)
> - No qualifying, no "but" -- pure celebration
> - Leader starts by modeling: specific, measurable, celebratory
>
> **9:05-9:15 | METRICS REVIEW (Team Lead)**
> - Team scorecard: listings, pendings, closings, new leads, appointments
> - vs. weekly and monthly targets
> - Individual numbers (displayed, not called out -- nobody gets embarrassed publicly)
> - Highlight: who is above target (recognize), who needs support (offer privately after meeting)
>
> **9:15-9:25 | DEAL DEEP DIVE (All)**
> - Each agent flags deals that are stuck or at risk (2 minutes per deal max)
> - Format: "Property address, what is stuck, what I have tried, what I need from the team"
> - Team brainstorms solutions -- one suggestion per person, no debating
> - Agent chooses which suggestion to act on
>
> **9:25-9:35 | TRAINING OR TOPIC OF THE WEEK (Rotating Lead)**
> - 10-minute deep dive on ONE topic (rotating: scripts, contracts, marketing, tech, market update)
> - Format: "Here is the problem / here is the solution / here is how to use it this week"
> - This week's topic: [INSERT]
> - This week's lead: [INSERT]
>
> **9:35-9:45 | ACCOUNTABILITY CHECK (All)**
> - Review last week's commitments: Did you do what you said you would do?
> - If not: What got in the way? What will you do differently this week?
> - This is not about shame. It is about patterns. Missed commitments two weeks in a row = a private conversation about what is actually going on.
>
> **9:45-9:55 | THIS WEEK'S PRIORITIES (Individual)**
> - Each person states their ONE most important priority for this week
> - "This week is a win if I _______."
> - Specific, measurable, within their control
> - Written down by the team lead
>
> **9:55-10:00 | CLOSE + STANDARD REMINDERS (Team Lead)**
> - Upcoming deadlines, events, training
> - Schedule shifts or coverage needs
> - Positive close: one sentence that reminds the team why we do this work
>
> ---
>
> **GROUND RULES**
> 1. Phones face down. Laptops closed unless presenting.
> 2. Start on time. Late arrivals donate $10 to the team coffee fund.
> 3. No problem without a proposed solution. If you raise an issue, come with at least one idea.
> 4. Celebrate wins. Complain offline.
> 5. Action items are commitments, not suggestions. Own them or negotiate them.

### BUSINESS PROMPT 5: Goal Setting (Quarterly)

**When To Use:** Quarterly goal-setting session. This prompt helps you articulate specific, measurable goals across all areas of your business and create an action plan for achieving them.

**Context To Gather:**
- Previous quarter's performance (closed volume, GCI, transactions, new leads, etc.)
- Annual goals (to ensure quarterly goals align)
- Current pipeline (active listings, pending transactions, active buyers)
- Areas of the business that need attention (lead gen, conversion, systems, team, etc.)
- Personal goals that intersect with business (vacation, family time, health, etc.)
- Market outlook for the coming quarter

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate business coach guiding an agent through a quarterly goal-setting session. Your approach balances ambition with realism: set goals that stretch without breaking. Every goal must have a plan, not just a number.

Guide the agent through a structured quarterly goal-setting exercise. The output should be a complete quarterly plan with these sections:

1. LAST QUARTER DEBRIEF (3-5 sentences on what worked, what didn't, key lessons)
2. Q[X] BIG 3 -- the three goals that define success for this quarter (transactional, financial, personal)
3. LEADING INDICATORS -- the activities that will produce the results (calls, appointments, listings taken, etc.)
4. MONTHLY MILESTONES -- interim targets for each month
5. OBSTACLE FORECAST -- what is most likely to derail you and how you will handle it
6. SUPPORT & ACCOUNTABILITY -- who knows your goals, when you will check in, what happens if you hit/miss

Rules:
- Every goal needs a specific number and a deadline
- Goals must be within the agent's control (not "sell 10 homes" but "take 4 listings, write 8 offers, prospect 50 hours")
- Include one personal/human goal (not everything is about work)
- The plan should be motivating, not overwhelming
- Length: 1-2 pages when printed
- Tone: Ambitious but grounded. A coach who believes in you and holds you accountable.

QUARTERLY DETAILS:
- Last Quarter Performance: [INSERT key numbers]
- Annual Goals: [INSERT]
- Current Pipeline: [INSERT]
- Areas Needing Attention: [INSERT]
- Personal Goals: [INSERT]
- Market Outlook: [INSERT]
- Quarter: [INSERT Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 YEAR]
```

### BUSINESS PROMPT 6: Database Reactivation Campaign

**When To Use:** Creating a campaign to re-engage past clients and dormant contacts in your database. The goal is to generate conversations, not transactions -- transactions come from conversations.

**Context To Gather:**
- Database size and segments (past clients, sphere of influence, cold leads, etc.)
- How long since last meaningful contact
- The offer or hook (market update, home value estimate, event invitation, helpful resource)
- Campaign duration and touch frequency
- Channel strategy (email, text, phone, direct mail, social media)
- Follow-up plan for contacts who respond

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate marketing strategist designing a database reactivation campaign. Your goal is to restart dormant relationships and identify who in your database is planning to transact in the next 12 months. This is not a sales blast -- it is a strategic outreach campaign that feels personal and valuable.

Design a database reactivation campaign. Include:

1. CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW (goal, duration, target audience segments)
2. SEGMENTATION STRATEGY (how you divide your database for different messaging)
3. TOUCH SEQUENCE (multi-channel, timed, with specific messaging for each touch)
4. THE HOOK (what value are you offering that makes people respond?)
5. RESPONSE HANDLING (what to do when someone replies -- scripts for common responses)
6. SUCCESS METRICS (what numbers will you track?)

Rules:
- Segment your database: past clients vs. cold leads vs. SOI get different messages
- Every touch must provide value first, ask second
- The "offer" should be something people actually want (a free CMA, a market update, a neighborhood guide, a home value estimate)
- Include a phone call component -- do not hide behind email
- Have a follow-up plan: what happens after someone responds?
- Length: Detailed campaign outline
- Tone: Strategic, systematic, personal at scale.

DATABASE DETAILS:
- Total Contacts: [INSERT]
- Segments: [INSERT breakdown]
- Last Contact: [INSERT how long since meaningful outreach]
- Campaign Goal: [INSERT conversations/reconnections, not transactions]
- Duration: [INSERT weeks/months]
- Offer/Hook: [INSERT value proposition for responding]
```

### BUSINESS PROMPT 7: New Agent Onboarding Guide

**When To Use:** Creating an onboarding document or training plan for a new agent joining your team. This sets expectations, provides a clear path, and accelerates their path to productivity.

**Context To Gather:**
- New agent's background (newly licensed, experienced, switching brokerages, etc.)
- Team role and expectations (buyer agent, showing assistant, full-service agent, etc.)
- Commission split and compensation structure
- Training resources available (in-house, online, mentorship, etc.)
- Technology stack they need to learn
- Timeline to first transaction
- Team culture and values

**The Prompt:**

```
You are a real estate team leader creating an onboarding plan for a new agent. The plan should make them feel supported, set clear expectations, and give them a concrete path to their first paycheck. Onboarding is the most important retention tool you have -- get it right and they stay for years. Get it wrong and they are gone in 90 days.

Write a new agent onboarding guide. Include:

1. WELCOME MESSAGE (team culture + what success looks like here)
2. FIRST 30 DAYS (week-by-week breakdown: what to learn, what to do, what to produce)
3. TECHNOLOGY CHECKLIST (every tool they need access to and training on)
4. TRAINING & MENTORSHIP (who teaches them what, how feedback works)
5. PERFORMANCE MILESTONES (what defines "on track" at 30, 60, 90 days)
6. COMPENSATION CLARITY (how they get paid, when they get paid, what to expect)
7. CULTURE & NORMS (how the team communicates, expectations around availability, meetings, collaboration)

Rules:
- Be specific about week-by-week expectations (do not just say "learn the CRM")
- Include actual numbers for performance milestones
- The first week should include a quick win (something they can do that produces a result fast)
- Be honest about the ramp-up time and income reality (do not overpromise)
- Make it clear that help is available and asking questions is expected
- Length: 2-3 pages when printed
- Tone: Welcoming but clear. Supportive but with standards. You want them to succeed and you have built the path.

ONBOARDING DETAILS:
- New Agent Name: [INSERT]
- Background: [INSERT newly licensed, experienced, etc.]
- Role: [INSERT]
```
