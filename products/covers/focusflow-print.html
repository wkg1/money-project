Quick Start Guide (5 Minutes)

Step 1: Duplicate the Template

Click the link above and hit Duplicate in Notion. This creates your own copy that you can customize freely.

Step 2: Start With a Brain Dump

Go to the 🧠 Brain Dump database. Write down EVERYTHING on your mind right now — tasks, worries, ideas, random thoughts. Don't filter. Don't organize. Just dump. This takes about 3 minutes and clears mental RAM immediately.

Step 3: Pick Your 3 Focus Tasks

Go to ✅ Today's Focus. Pick just THREE things. Not 10. Not 5. Three. Match them to your energy level — high-energy tasks for morning, low-energy tasks for afternoon.

💡 ADHD Tip: If you can't decide which 3 tasks, pick the ones you're most anxious about. Getting them done first kills two birds: progress + relief.

Step 4: Track Your Dopamine

Go to 🎮 Dopamine Tracker at the end of your day. Don't track what you DIDN'T do. Only track what you DID. Every small win counts — even "got out of bed on time" is a win.

The 3 Databases Explained

🧠 Brain Dump

Your external hard drive. ADHD brains have limited working memory — when you try to hold everything in your head, nothing gets done. Brain Dump is where thoughts go to stop spinning.

  • Open it any time a thought pops up
  • Auto-categorizes into: Ideas / Tasks / Worries / Inspiration
  • Review daily to clear the queue

✅ Today's Focus

The anti-overwhelm tool. Most to-do lists fail because they show 50 items, and your brain sees "50 things I haven't done" and shuts down. Today's Focus shows you exactly 3 things.

  • Only 3 tasks visible at a time
  • Energy-level matching (high/medium/low)
  • "Already started" button for when perfectionism blocks you

🎮 Dopamine Tracker

ADHD brains need external rewards. Neurotypical brains get dopamine from completing tasks — ADHD brains often don't. This tracker builds a visual record of your wins so you can SEE your progress.

  • Weekly achievement wall
  • Mood tracking (spot your patterns)
  • Reward menu — pick what you've earned

Why This Works (The Neuroscience)

Traditional productivity advice assumes you have normal dopamine function. ADHD brains process motivation differently:

  • Novelty → Dopamine: New systems feel great for about 3 days. Then the dopamine fades and you abandon them. FocusFlow counters this with daily visual feedback — small wins, every day.
  • Choice Paralysis: Too many options = zero decisions. Three tasks = you actually start one.
  • Externalize Everything: Your brain is for thinking, not storing. Brain Dump does the storing.
