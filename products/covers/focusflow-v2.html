A complete step-by-step guide to building your own 3-database Notion system — designed for how ADHD brains actually work.

Welcome

If you have ADHD, you have probably tried every productivity system out there. Bullet journals. GTD. Pomodoro. Complex Notion templates with 47 interconnected databases.

They all work great — for about three days. Then the novelty wears off, the dopamine fades, and you are back to square one, feeling like you failed again.

You did not fail. The system failed you.

Most productivity tools are built for neurotypical brains. They assume you can: look at a list of 50 tasks without feeling paralyzed; remember to check your task manager without external cues; get dopamine from completing a checkbox. ADHD brains do not work that way. This guide will show you how to build a system that actually works with your brain, not against it.

The Three Principles

1. Externalize Everything

Your working memory is limited. When you try to hold tasks, ideas, and worries in your head simultaneously, nothing gets done — your brain spends all its energy trying not to forget. The solution: get everything out of your head and into an external system immediately. No filtering. No organizing. Just dump.

2. Limit Choices to Three

Choice paralysis is real. Faced with 20 tasks, an ADHD brain sees "20 things I have not done" and shuts down. But three tasks? Three is manageable. Three feels possible. Three you can actually start.

3. Manufacture Dopamine

Neurotypical brains release dopamine when completing tasks. ADHD brains often do not — the reward system works differently. You need to see your progress visually. A wall of completed tasks, a streak of good days, a visual record that proves you are making progress — even when it does not feel like it.

Building Your FocusFlow System in Notion (Step by Step)

We are going to build three simple Notion databases. No templates to buy, no complex formulas — just step-by-step instructions. You will need a free Notion account at notion.so. Each database takes about 5 minutes to set up.

Database 1: 🧠 Brain Dump

Purpose: Capture any thought in under 3 seconds before it escapes.

How to create: In Notion, create a new page called "Brain Dump". Type /database and select "Database - Inline". Name it "Brain Dump".

Fields to add:

Name (default Title field) — the thought itself
Type (Select) — options: 💡 Idea, ✅ Task, 😰 Worry, ✨ Inspiration
Status (Select) — options: New, Processing, Done, Dismissed
Created (Created time) — automatic timestamp

Views:

Quick Capture — Table view, sorted newest first. Your main dumping ground.
Worries — Filter: Type = 😰 Worry AND Status = New. Review each morning.
Ideas — Filter: Type = 💡 Idea AND Status = New. Review weekly.
💡 How to use: Brain Dump is not a todo list. It is a parking lot. The moment a thought pops into your head, open this page and type it. Three seconds max. Review once in the morning and once before bed.

Database 2: ✅ Today's Focus

Purpose: Show you exactly 3 tasks per day — no more, no less.

How to create: Create a new page called "Today's Focus". Type /database and select "Database - Inline".

Fields to add:

Task (Title) — what you need to do
Energy (Select) — ⚡ High, 🔋 Medium, 🪫 Low
Status (Select) — Not Started, In Progress, Done, Skipped
Date (Date) — when you plan to do it
Started? (Checkbox) — did you at least begin? (Most important field!)

Views:

Today — Gallery view, filter: Date = Today. Shows only 3 tasks at a time.
This Week — Calendar view, all tasks across the week.
Done! — Table view, filter: Status = Done. Your achievement wall.
💡 The "Started?" checkbox is the secret weapon. ADHD brains struggle with task initiation. This checkbox gives you a dopamine hit for simply beginning — even if you do not finish. Research shows once you start a task, you are 80% more likely to complete it.

Database 3: 🎮 Dopamine Tracker

Purpose: Build a visual record of your wins. See your progress. Stay motivated.

How to create: Create a new page called "Dopamine Tracker". Type /database and select "Database - Inline".

Fields to add:

Date (Date) — the day
What I Did (Title) — one thing you accomplished (no matter how small)
Mood (Select) — 😄 Great, 🙂 Good, 😐 Okay, 😕 Meh, 😞 Rough
Category (Select) — Work, Health, Relationships, Personal, Learning
Win Level (Select) — 🏆 Big Win, ⭐ Medium Win, 💪 Small Win

Views:

Win Wall — Gallery view, sorted newest first. Every win, big and small.
Mood Calendar — Calendar view by date. Spot patterns in your mood.
Weekly Summary — Table view, filter: This Week. Count your wins.
💡 Track wins, not losses. Only record what you DID. Not what you did not do. Even on your worst day, you did something — got out of bed, ate a meal, sent one email. Log it. It counts.

Your Daily Routine (5 Minutes Total)

Morning (2 min)

  1. Open Brain Dump → review Worries view. Any worries that are actually tasks? Move them to Today's Focus.
  2. Open Today's Focus → pick 3 tasks. Match them to your current energy level.
  3. Start the first task. Do not plan more. Do not optimize.

Evening (3 min)

  1. Open Today's Focus → check "Started?" for anything you began. Mark one as Done if completed.
  2. Open Dopamine Tracker → add at least one entry. What did you do today?
  3. Open Brain Dump → dump anything still spinning in your head. Clear the cache before sleep.

FAQ

Q: What if I forget to use the system for a week?
A: Normal. Pick it back up. Do not try to "catch up" — just start from today. The system is resilient to gaps.

Q: Can I customize it?
A: Absolutely. Add colors, emojis, more views, additional fields. Make it feel like yours. Just do not add more databases — three is the sweet spot.

Q: Why Notion and not a paper journal?
A: Paper journals require you to have the journal with you. Notion syncs to your phone. ADHD brains need to capture thoughts the moment they happen.

Q: Is this only for people with diagnosed ADHD?
A: Designed for ADHD brains, but anyone who struggles with focus, procrastination, or feeling overwhelmed will benefit.
